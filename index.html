Abstract

Electromagnetic side-channel leakage and EM injection are typically treated as separate phenomena. InjectEave shows that injected carriers can be modulated by nonlinear hardware, amplifying leakage that would otherwise be infeasible. This enables eavesdropping on audio and low-frequency analog secrets, even through walls, and introduces closed-loop manipulation of voice interfaces.

Key Contributions

  • Injection-Induced EM Side Channels as a unified framework for active leakage amplification.
  • End-to-end eavesdropping design combining EM injection, spectral capture, and diffusion-based speech enhancement.
  • Evaluation across 11 COTS devices with through-wall audio recovery and activity inference.

Mitigation & Limitations

Traditional EM hardening and digital obfuscation are insufficient against injection-induced leakage. The most promising defenses focus on detecting active injection and hardening analog interfaces post-decryption.

EM Hardening

Shielding, filtering, and twisted-pair cabling reduce coupling, but can be offset by higher injection power.

Conventional SCA Defenses

Masking and clock randomization protect digital logic but do not address analog leaks.

Injection Detection

Spectrum monitoring and anomaly detection offer promise, but introduce cost and operational complexity.
